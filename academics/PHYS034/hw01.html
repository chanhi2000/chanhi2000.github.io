1. Q-1.10

Suppose that A′A'A′, B′B'B′, and C′C'C′ are at rest in frame S′S'S′, which moves with respect to SSS at speed vvv in the +x+x+x direction. Let B′B'B′ be located exactly midway between A′A'A′ and C′C'C′. At t′=0t'=0t′=0, a light flash occurs at B′B'B′ and expands outward as a spherical wave.

(a)

According to an observer in S′S'S′, do the wave fronts arrive at A′A'A′ and C′C'C′ simultaneously?

(a) - Answer

According to S′S'S′ frame, the wave fronts arrive simultaneously at A′A'A′, and C′C'C′.

(b)

According to an observer in SSS, do the wave fronts arrive at A′A'A′ and C′C'C′ simultaneously?

(b) - Answer

According to S′S'S′ frame, the wave fronts DO NOT arrive simultaneously at A′A'A′, and C′C'C′.
